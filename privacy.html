Our policy is that you can trust Monoidentity because you don't have to. There aren't any blind boxes you need to put your faith in.

A private baseline

An app that uses Monoidentity is private by default, with anything you enter in monoidentity.web.app being transferred directly to the app, and anything you do in the app staying entirely local. You can read our code to verify this.

The verification primitive

If an app wants to verify you, we have a simple method for this: connect directly to StudentVue to exchange your password for a token. That token goes to our server to verify you're who you say you are (and if successful, generating a JWT). We don't store the input, details, or output of this process.

The cloud primitive

Any user can opt to get verified and get sponsored with a cloud storage bucket hosted on R2. Data that is synced - chosen by apps, and excluding personal data like passwords and caches - isn't end to end encrypted, but our policy is that it's a. sent over HTTPS and sent directly b. encrypted at rest c. for you only (but that goes without saying).

No tracking

No tracking cookies, no ads, no analytics.
